May 2025

Self-Balancing Quadruped Robot with Modular Electronics

Designed a 12-DOF quadruped platform using Denavit-Hartenberg modelling and FEA to ensure stability and durability within a £180 bill-of-materials budget.

  • Produced 25+ Autodesk Inventor drawings with GD&T tolerances of ±0.05 mm.
  • Built inverse kinematics solver in C++ achieving <5 ms compute time and ±2 mm accuracy.
  • Developed gait generator and state machine control for stabilizing movement on surface tilts.
  • Implemented sensor fusion (α = 0.98, 100 Hz) and Butterworth low-pass filtering (20 Hz cutoff).
  • Tuned PID (Kp 2.5, Ki 0.1, Kd 0.05) delivering ±3° pitch/roll stability.
  • Integrated ESP32 + MPU6050 PID control, improving uneven terrain stability by 30% in testing.
  • Calibrated servo actuation via PCA9685 with femur-tibia mirroring logic for dynamic CoG control.
  • Structured modular firmware with optimized PWM constraints for energy efficiency.
